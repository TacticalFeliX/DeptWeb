Research Facilities

The department boasts several state-of-the-art research facilities that support cutting-edge work in condensed matter physics, materials science, and interdisciplinary domains. Below are some of our key laboratories and their capabilities.

X-Ray Diffraction (XRD) Facility

Equipment: High-resolution X-ray diffractometer with temperature control and in-situ capabilities.

Capabilities:

  • Phase identification of crystalline materials
  • Crystallite size and microstrain analysis
  • Texture and residual stress studies
  • Thin film characterization using GIXRD and reflectometry

Applications: Structural analysis in materials science, metallurgy, and nanotechnology.

Raman Spectroscopy Facility

Equipment: Multi-laser Raman spectrometer with high sensitivity CCD detectors.

Capabilities:

  • Bonding and molecular structure analysis
  • Strain/stress mapping in thin films and semiconductors
  • Raman imaging and temperature-dependent studies
  • Characterization of carbon nanostructures, 2D materials

Applications: Nanotechnology, optoelectronics, polymer science, and chemistry.

UV-Visible Spectroscopy Facility

Equipment: High-resolution UV-Vis spectrometer suitable for both solids and liquids.

Capabilities:

  • Optical absorbance and transmittance measurements
  • Band gap determination using Tauc plots
  • Concentration estimation using Beer-Lambert law
  • Time-resolved measurements and film thickness estimation

Applications: Photonics, materials research, chemical sensing, and environmental studies.

High-Performance Computing (HPC) Facility

Hardware: Multi-core CPU and GPU clusters with high RAM and fast interconnects.

Capabilities:

  • First-principles simulations (DFT, ab-initio methods)
  • Molecular dynamics and Monte Carlo simulations
  • Machine learning in materials discovery
  • Data-intensive analysis of experimental results

Applications: Theoretical physics, computational chemistry, statistical modeling, and AI in materials science.
